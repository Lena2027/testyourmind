International Women’s Day: Celebrating in Berlin and Mecklenburg-Vorpommern

March 8th, International Women’s Day (Frauentag), is a special public holiday in Germany, but only in two specific states.

Which States Celebrate?

For a long time, Berlin was the only German state to recognize International Women’s Day as an official public holiday (since 2019). However, starting in 2023, Mecklenburg-Vorpommern joined Berlin, making it a legal holiday in both regions. In the rest of Germany, it is observed as an important day of awareness but remains a regular working day.

The History of Frauentag in Germany

The decision to make March 8th a public holiday in these states was driven by a desire to promote gender equality and acknowledge the historical struggles for women's rights. In the capital city of Berlin and the coastal state of Mecklenburg-Vorpommern, residents enjoy a day off to attend rallies, workshops, and cultural events.

Planning Tip: If you are traveling between states on March 8th, be aware that shops will be closed in Berlin and Schwerin, but open in neighboring Brandenburg or Schleswig-Holstein. Check the 2026 Calendar to see how this holiday fits into your schedule.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is March 8th a holiday in Munich or Hamburg?
A: No, it is currently only a public holiday in Berlin and Mecklenburg-Vorpommern.

Q: Are schools closed?
A: Yes, in the two states where it is a public holiday, schools and public offices are closed.
