A Beginner’s Guide to German Public Holidays (Feiertage)

Germany is known for its efficiency, but it also takes its holidays very seriously. If you're new to Germany, understanding 'Feiertage' is essential for survival.

What are Feiertage?

Public holidays in Germany, known as Gesetzliche Feiertage, are days when most businesses, supermarkets, and government offices close. Unlike some countries where holidays simply mean a day off work, in Germany, it often means a complete shutdown of the retail sector.

The Federal Structure

One of the most confusing aspects for beginners is that holidays vary by state. While there are 9 national holidays, states like Bavaria have significantly more due to Catholic traditions.

Survival Tips for Beginners

  • Stock up early: Supermarkets will be crowded the day before a holiday.
  • Quiet hours: Respect the 'Ruhezeit' – noise should be kept to a minimum.
  • Bakeries: Some bakeries might open for a few hours in the morning, but don't count on it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are restaurants open?
A: Generally, yes. Most restaurants and cafes remain open on public holidays.

Q: Do buses run?
A: Yes, but usually on a Sunday/holiday schedule, which is less frequent.
